## Why this exists

### What’s wrong with the web now

The modern web looks useful at first glance… but most of it isn’t.

Pages written to rank instead of say anything.  
AI content with no point of view.  
Affiliate sites pretending to help.  

You click… skim… leave… and remember nothing.

### What this is trying to bring back

The web is better when you can still stumble across something real.

A weird personal blog.  
A proper opinion.  
A niche site someone clearly cares about.  

Something that feels like a person made it.

<Aside type="caution" title="What you won’t find here">
No AI content, affiliate spam, or pages written just to rank.
</Aside>

---

## How sites get added

### The process

There’s no automation here.

Everything is done by hand.

A site gets found, checked, then placed where it actually fits.  
Descriptions are written properly — not generated.

### Why it’s slower (and better)

It moves slower on purpose.

<Aside type="tip" title="The rule">
If it doesn’t feel human, it doesn’t go on the site.
</Aside>

## Simple stats, nothing more

This site uses a **self-hosted GoatCounter instance** to get a rough idea of how busy things are.

There’s no tracking across sites, no profiling, and no attempt to follow you around.

Just basic, privacy-friendly stats so I can see what people are actually using and improve it.

<Aside type="tip" title="Why GoatCounter">
Open-source, lightweight, and built to respect privacy.
</Aside>
