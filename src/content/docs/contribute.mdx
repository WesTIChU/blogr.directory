## Contributing

### Submit a site

The easiest way is through GitHub:

👉 [Submit a site](https://github.com/WesTIChU/blogr.directory/issues/new/choose)

You’ll be asked for a few simple things:

Site name  
URL  
Category  
A short description  
Why it belongs here  

I go through every submission myself before anything gets added.

If you’d rather not use GitHub, you can also send it by email.

Just put together the same details and send it to:

add [at] blogr [dot] directory

---

### Improve an existing page

Spotted something off?

A typo, a broken link, or even just a better way to describe something — feel free to fix it.

At the bottom of each page, you’ll find an **“Edit this page on GitHub”** link.

Clicking that will:

- Open the page in GitHub  
- Let you make changes directly in your browser  
- Create a suggested edit (pull request)  

I’ll review it and approve it if it looks good.

You don’t need to be a developer — just make the change and submit it.

---

### Report AI-generated content

Even with manual checks, things can slip through.

If you come across a site in the directory that feels AI-generated, low-effort, or out of place, you can report it.

👉 [Report a site](https://github.com/WesTIChU/blogr.directory/issues/new/choose)

You don’t need to write anything complicated, just send the link and a quick note.

I’ll review it properly and remove anything that doesn’t belong.

This site is curated by a human, not a system, so a second pair of eyes helps a lot.

---

## How links are checked

Every site gets a quick manual check before it’s listed.

I’ll usually:

- Visit the site and make sure it’s working properly  
- Check for recent posts or activity  
- Look for signs that it’s still maintained  

If there aren’t clear dates on the site, I’ll check the RSS feed to see when it was last updated.

This helps keep the directory full of active, genuinely maintained sites.

That said, older or inactive sites can still be included if the content is genuinely worth reading.

## Guidelines

### What gets accepted

Personal blogs and websites  
Independent writers  
Small communities and forums  
Thoughtful, original content  
Anything with a clear human voice  

Sites built with modern tools are completely fine too.  
That includes AI helping with layout, design, or structure.

What matters is the writing behind it.

---

### What won’t be added

Fully AI-written content farms  
Affiliate spam or SEO churn sites  
Generic “top 10” blog networks  
Corporate publishing platforms  
Low-effort or clearly abandoned sites  

---

### A few simple guidelines

Keep descriptions short and honest  
Check it’s not already listed  
Make sure the site is active  
Link to the actual site, not just social profiles  

---

## AI & tools

I’m not against AI.

If anything, it’s helped more people get online who maybe wouldn’t have had the tools or skills before — and I think that’s a good thing.

If your site uses AI for layout, code, or structure, that’s completely fine.

But the content itself should be human.

Real thoughts.  
Real experiences.  
A real voice behind it.

That’s the line.

---

## Why this exists

The modern internet is full of noise.

AI content, recycled articles, and platforms designed to keep you scrolling.

I wanted something quieter.

A more human web, where people write, share, and build things that actually mean something.
